4 SEASONS
The passing of a year can bring a marked change in the weather and the surrounding environment. The four seasons — winter, spring, summer, autumn — can vary significantly in characteristics, and can prompt changes in the world around them.
Spring: Seeds take root and vegetation begins to grow. The weather is warmer, and often wetter.
Summer: Temperatures may increase to their hottest of the year. If they spike too high, heat waves or droughts may cause trouble for people, animals, and plants.
Autumn: Temperatures cool again. Plants may begin to grow dormant. Animals might prepare themselves for the upcoming cold weather, storing food or traveling to warmer regions. Various cultures have celebrated bountiful harvests with annual festivals.
Winter: It often brings a chill. Some areas may experience snow or ice, while others see only cold rain.
